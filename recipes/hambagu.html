Japanese Hamburger Steak (Hambagu)

Description

Hambagu or Japanese Hamburger Steak has been around since the 1800's. It is a yoshoku dish, or in other words, a Japanese dish that has been influenced by Western cuisine. This is a savory dish that many Japanese claim as a comfort food. Let's explore this famous dish and bring a taste of Japanese comfort to your friends and family!

Ingredients

  • 1/2 Onion
  • 1 Tbsp Neutral Flavored Oil
  • 1 lb Ground Beef or 1 lb 50-50 Ground Beef and Ground Pork
  • 1/2 tsp Kosher Salt
  • Freshly Ground Black Pepper
  • 1/2 tsp Nutmeg
  • 1/3 cup Panko
  • 2 Tbsp Milk
  • 1 Large Egg
  • 1 Tbsp Unsalted Butter
  • 3 Tbsp Ketchup
  • 3 Tbsp Tonkatsu Sauce
  • 3 Tbsp Red Wine
  • 3 Tbsp Water>

Steps

  1. Finely mince onion.
  2. Heat oil in pan.
  3. Sautee onion until soft.
  4. Put onion aside in bowl and allow to cool.
  5. Add ground meat, salt, pepper, nutmeg, panko, and egg.
  6. Mix ingredients with spatula or spoon.
  7. Switch to hand mixing and kneading until mixture is sticky and pale.
  8. Divide mixture into quarters.
  9. Scoop out one portion and toss from hand to hand. (It gets the air out of the patty so it maintains shape while being cooked!)
  10. Shape patty into an oval shape and place on a tray.
  11. Continue process with the rest of the portions.
  12. Refrigerate patties for 30 minutes so fat solidifies.
  13. Heat oil in pan over medium heat.
  14. Indent center of patties. (The centers will rise with heat).
  15. Cook on patties for 3 minutes on both sides.
  16. Add 3 Tbsp red wine and turn heat to low.
  17. Cover patties and steam in wine for 5-7 minutes.
  18. Uncover and poke patties with skewer. If juices run clear, the patties are finished cooking.
  19. Raise to medium heat and allow wine to burn off.
  20. We aren't done yet!
  21. Add unsalted butter, ketchup, tonkatsu sauce, 3 Tbsp red wint, and water to pan.
  22. Mix well and bring to a simmer over medium heat and allow red wine to evaporate.
  23. Sauce will thicken enough that you can draw an line across bottom of pan with a spatula or spoon.
  24. Remove from heat and drizzle over steak patties.
  25. Now for the most important part: Enjoy!
